    USC Sustainability Progress Report

    Review USC’s progress from this past academic year around its efforts to make a more sustainable future for our campus and planet.

    1.5 million single-use plastic beverage bottles prevented from entering the waste stream

    By Earth Day 2023, USC will have prevented over 1.5 million single-use plastic beverage bottles from entering the waste stream since university enacted the ban. USC is on its way to achieving Assignment: Earth’s goal of zero waste by 2028. Find out how you can help.

    USC Sustainability Hub to open in Fall 2023

    The new hub will be housed in the Student Union building and will be a space for collaboration and research focused on Assignment: Earth initiatives.

    Highlights

    • IYA “Scrap” Book: Free Materials, Freer Mind
      There’s no shortage of original creations and tangible works of innovation in a variety of materials at USC Iovine and Young Academy, but there’s also no shortage of the excess these constructions leave in their wake.
    • Beyond Earth Month: Undergraduate scholars tackle environmental health challenges across Southern California
      Undergraduate students in the EH MATTERS fellowship program are committed to improving the health and wellness of communities here in Los Angeles.
    • Integrating Innovative Climate Education into Curriculum
      In commemoration of Earth Day, students enrolled in the Health Promotion 448: Global Environmental Changes and Health course, wrote op-eds about the intersectionality of various public health issues in relation to climate change.
    • Trending Towards Sustainability: The Next Generation of Fashion
      Is sustainability really the new black? Trends indicate sustainability is a rising factor in fashion.
