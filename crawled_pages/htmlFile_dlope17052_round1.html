Add to Calendar 2023-03-02 16:00:00 2023-05-30 17:00:00 The Vanishing Worlds of Audubon When John James Audubon was working on his four-volume Birds of America in the 1820s1830s, ornithological conservation of any kind was hardly discussed, particularly when the birds he described seemed so overwhelmingly abundant: The air was literally filled with pigeons; the light of noon-day was obscured as by an eclipse. Since then, human threats to avian wildlife have mounted, as many species have been hunted to extinction or near-extinction for food, sport, and fashionor threatened by urban development, climate change, and overuse of fertilizers and pesticides.
