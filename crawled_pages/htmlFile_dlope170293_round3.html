Passport Place

Passport Appointment Information

Before you schedule an appointment... 

We provide passport services for the following cases: 

  1. New applicants
  2. Minors age 0-15 (new or renewal)
  3. Adult renewals (16+) only in one of the following cases:
    • Your passport has been expired over 5 years
    • You are going from a minor to an adult passport (passports which are valid for 5 years are considered minor passports)
    • Your previous passport was lost or stolen (include DS-64 in your application or complete it online) or if it's been damaged or mutilated.

All other renewals must be done by mail directly to the National Passport Processing Center. See instructions below under Form DS-82.  (PDF)

We have transitioned to APPOINTMENTS ONLY. At the moment, our appointments are only bookable 2 weeks out. We recommend booking early in the morning as slots fill up quickly and there often isn't availability unless someone cancels their appointment.

 Click Here to Schedule a Passport Appointment

Planning on traveling or studying abroad and in need of a U.S. passport? You can now submit an application for a passport right on the Chico State Campus!

The Passport Place was established in March of 2019 and is located in the International Education and Global Engagement (IEGE) Office, Student Services Center Building, Room 440, located on the corner of West 2nd and Ivy streets.  The Passport Place is open to all Chico State students, faculty, and staff as well as any U.S. Citizens!

Please note that parking on campus is very limited, so allow yourself adequate time to locate a parking space.

Please take note of current passport processing times. Routine service takes 10-13 weeks, and expedited processing (for an additional $60) takes 7-9 weeks. 

It may take up to 2 weeks for applications to arrive by mail at a passport agency or center, and up to 2 weeks for you to receive a completed passport in the mail after we print it.

Applying for a Passport 

To apply for a United States Passport, you will need to follow these steps:

  1. Complete Form DS-11  (PDF): Application for A U.S. Passport - Black ink only, or printed single-sided (which also can be obtained in person at the Passport Place in SSC 440). Application must be completed prior to appointment time. 
  2. Submit Completed Form DS-11 In-Person – Student Support Services Building, Room 440
  3. Provide Evidence of U.S. Citizenship - Original or Certified Birth Certificate, Naturalization Certificate, or previous U.S. passport (Please bring original and one photocopy*). 
  4. Present Government Issued Identification - Photo ID (Driver's License, State ID, Military ID, etc.) and a photocopy* of front and back. 
  5. Pay the Applicable Fees (two separate payments)—two checks (personal, certified, cashier's, traveler's), OR two money orders only—NO CASH OR CREDIT CARD ACCEPTED (see fee chart below)
  6. Provide One Passport Photo - NOT a service provided by the Chico State Passport Place
  • Minors (under 16) must be present with BOTH parents. 
    • If the child only has one parent/legal guardian, evidence of sole custody of the child must be submitted with the application.
    • Proof of parental relationship (Ex. Certified Birth Certificate, etc.) is required for all minor applicants (under 16) even if previous passport is submitted with DS-11 application.

*(copies can be made for a 25 cent fee + tax)

Getting a Passport Photo

The Passport Place at Chico State is NOT a location where you are able to get your passport photo taken.  There are certain parameters set forth by the Department of State.

  • For more information on passport photos, please go to the Department of State website.
  • There are several places in Chico where you can go to get an appropriately sized photo.

Note: The application process must be completed in person. No part of the process can be completed by email or over the telephone. Applicant must be present to apply.

Passport Forms

DS-11 - First time Applicants
  • Applying for a U.S. passport book or card for the first time
  • Current U.S. passport is valid but has been lost, stolen or damaged
  • Previous U.S. passport was issued more than 15 years ago
(PDF)
DS-64 - Lost or Stolen Passport
  • Used to report the loss or theft of a valid U.S. passport book or card
  • Submitted with Form DS-11 and supporting documents to replace a previously lost or stolen passport
  • Used alone to report the loss or theft of a passport even if you do not want a replacement passport
(PDF)
DS-3053 - Non-appearing Parent for Minors
  • Completed by the non-appearing parent for minor applicants under the age of 16
  • Must be notarized within the last 90 days, and must be accompanied by a copy of the non-appearing parent's photo ID
  • Submitted with Form DS-11
(PDF)
DS-82 - Passport Renewal
  • To renew an existing, 10-year passport
  • To replace a limited-validity passport that is more than 1 year old
  • Only use if current passport was issued less than 15 years ago
  • Only use if name on the application and the previous passport are the exact same (unless you are submitting a legal name change document or marriage certificate)
  • Only if the passport is undamaged and can be submitted by mail with Form DS-82
  • Passport renewals do not require a visit to a Passport Acceptance Facility. Renewals can be mailed to the appropriate address listed on the DS-82.
  • In order to protect the contents of your mailing from the elements throughout the delivery process, it is strongly recommended that you mail your passport application and personal documents in an envelope large enough to fit the application without folding. Please do not use a standard letter-size #10 (4 1/8" x 9 1/2") envelope.
(PDF)
DS-5504 - Passport Corrections
  • To request a name change
  • To correct data errors (typos)
  • To replace limited validity passports (if eligible)
  • You may submit Form DS-5504 by mail
(PDF)
