CSUF COVID-19 Information & Resources

As of April 6, 2023, the CSU now strongly recommends that all individuals who access any in-person program or activity (on- or off-campus) operated or controlled by the University follow COVID-19 vaccine recommendations adopted by the U.S. Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC) and the California Department of Public Health (CDPH) applicable to their age, medical condition, and other relevant indications. In light of this change, as well as current guidance from the CDC, CDPH and Cal/OSHA, President’s Directive No. 22 will no longer be in effect as of April 17, 2023.

All university employees who test positive for COVID-19, have been in close contact with someone who has tested positive, or are experiencing symptoms they believe are related to COVID-19 are strongly encouraged to submit a COVID-19 Self-Reporting Form. No members of the university community should come to any university property or participate in any university-related activity if they are experiencing symptoms they believe are related to COVID-19. In addition, campus community members who develop symptoms they believe are related to COVID-19 while on University property or participating in any University-related activity should leave campus or remove themselves from the activity.
