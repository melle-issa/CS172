Construction Management

Graduates of Chico State’s Construction Management program (CMGT), are equipped to solve complex challenges in the construction industry. The program combines theoretical and applied learning taught by faculty managing all aspects of construction with decades of experience in the field.

From project managers to on-site superintendents, the CMGT program prepares students to become on-the-job leaders from day one.

What’s the Construction Management Program?

Housed in the College of Engineering, Chico State’s CMGT program transforms students’ curiosity to graduates ready to join a high-profile team of construction leaders out in the workplace. Our small class sizes and curriculum which blends theory with hands-on learning fosters the perfect learning environment for students to explore the intricacies of construction management.

Accredited by the American Council for Construction Education (ACCE), the CMGT program has nurtured deep and time-tested relationships with more than 60 leaders in the construction industry; leaders who have been at the forefront of the fast-paced construction industry, managing and building the structures we live, work and play in across the globe.

Career Paths for CMGT Graduates

Our reputation is solid. We’re proud to be the go-to construction management program for companies of all sizes, managing the construction of schools, hospitals, transportation infrastructure, and so much more.

The construction industry knows that graduates of Chico State’s CMGT program are ready to jump right and make an impact in careers such as:

  • Project Engineer
  • Field Engineer
  • Estimating Engineer
  • Assistant Superintendent
  • Specialty Engineer
  • Marketing and Project Procurement
  • Pre-Construction Engineer

What Do Students and Graduates of the CMGT Program Say?

To date, more than 3,000 students have crossed the stage and proudly share they are graduates of Chico State’s construction management program. Our graduates proudly wear that Wildcat CMGT badge of honor and continue the tradition of supporting the program and peers within the industry.
