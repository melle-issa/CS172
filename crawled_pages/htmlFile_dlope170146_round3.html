Description

Help us build a Pipeline to Success! We are passionate about providing first-generation, low-income, and other underserved undergraduate and graduate students the opportunity to reach their full potential through educational advancement and professional achievement.

To aid in pursuing these goals, we have established the Pipeline to Success Fund, which aims to reduce graduate students' financial burdens and encourage undergraduates to apply to graduate programs. Once enrolled in a program, the fund will serve to engage and support students in extracurricular research, conference presentations, and publishing opportunities that will prepare them for professional careers or doctoral degree programs.

How will donations be used?

Below are some costs our students face while pursuing their educational goals. Your support of any amount is greatly appreciated and will directly benefit our students.

  • $10–20 gas money for student travel to internships/field research
  • $70 covers the fee for one graduate school application
  • $100 pays for membership in a professional organization
  • $150 covers travel to a local conference
  • $200 pays for a professional conference registration
  • $500 covers costs for a national conference

Support the Pipeline to Success Fund!

A master's degree is the key to unlocking exclusive career opportunities, job security, and more significant earning potential! The Department of Labor and the US Bureau of Labor Statistics reports that those with a master's degree can access some of the fastest-growing career paths, such as health, social services, sciences, and computer technology. Plus, the average earnings for those with a master's degree are $500,000 more over a career than those with just a bachelor's degree.

Unfortunately, too few undergraduates across the state and at our own University are enrolling in graduate programs. In spring 2022, less than 5% of our undergraduates transitioned to graduate school. Less than a quarter of our graduate students are first-generation, low-income, or students of color. Why is this? One reason, according to students, is financial constraints. A more distressing reason is a lack of confidence in their own abilities, otherwise known as “imposter syndrome.”

Your support can help change this.

Another challenge to graduate school is that less funding is being funneled into programs to help students succeed and prepare them for professional careers or doctoral degree study. It has been shown that students who receive funding for their research, special projects, conference participation, and membership in professional organizations demonstrate noteworthy accomplishments frequently highlighted in campus and other significant publications. In recent years our students have published articles in peer-reviewed journals, won a national award for Outstanding Master’s Thesis, attained a Fulbright Fellowship, and received best scholarly paper awards at national and regional academic conferences. Many graduate students have gone on to earn a Ph.D. at prestigious universities.

Your support can help make sure the accomplishments of our students continue.

Why are donations necessary?

The bottom line, graduate students receive little University or other funding.
The first stumbling block many students face is the fee to apply to graduate school. Currently, there are no means to waive graduate program application fees to support undergraduates in need. This is an unfortunate deterrent for many students.

Second, once students are in a program, they face further funding challenges as they pursue professional development and academic achievement opportunities. The Office of Graduate Studies has a modest budget to support 8 to 10 graduate students in their research and conference attendance. With your help, more students can be supported, encouraged, and provided with more opportunities.

Your assistance will help to further diversify Chico State’s grad student population by supporting first-generation, low-income, and other underserved students by providing them with the means to waive the application fee and alleviate other financial concerns. This small token is a huge confidence booster for students—it signals to them that we see their potential and want to see them succeed academically and in life.

Your support will also help to build our small grants program for graduate students and promising undergraduates to pursue research, conference presentations, publications, and doctoral degree program admissions.

We believe in the University’s commitment to cultivating an inclusive campus where students have an equitable opportunity to thrive, where recruitment of diverse students is maximized, and activities are available to support an accessible, equitable, and transformative learning environment.

Join us in our commitment to achieving academic distinction, transformative student experiences, and prominent scholarship.
