Special Operating Funds

COAST (Council On Ocean Affairs, Science, and Technology)(opens in new window)

System-wide resources and promotion of interdisciplinary multi-campus collaborations to advance our knowledge of natural coast and marine resources and processes that affect them.

Fund: T8511

ARI (Agriculture Research Institute)(opens in new window)

Research and technology transfer activities complement the basic research conducted by the nation's land-grant universities and aim to improve the economic viability and sustainability of California agriculture.

Fund: T8512

WRPI (Water Resources and Policy Initiatives)(opens in new window)

Develops water management solutions through research, partnership, education and training that provides students with hands-on learning.

Fund: T8514

CSUPERB (CSU Program for Education and Research in Biotechnology)(opens in new window)

Develops professional biotechnology workforce by mobilizing and supporting collaborative CSU students and faculty research, innovating education practices, and responding to and anticipating the needs of the life science industry.

Fund: T8510

RSCA (Research Scholarly, and Creative Awards)(opens in new window)

These funds are only to be used in support of faculty research, scholarly and creative activities in accordance with article 25.6 of the collective bargaining agreement.

Fund: T8515

Other Funds

Lottery(opens in new window)

Generated from the sale of California State Lottery tickets, a portion of which is allocated to CSU campuses. Lottery funds must be used for instructional purposes only and must not supplement state funding of instruction.

22/23 Funding: $1.6 Million

IRA (Instructionally Related Activities)(opens in new window)

Generated by mandatory fees paid by all students as a part of their registration fees. These funds are used for out-of-classroom activities and experiences integrally related to the instructional offering, including athletics and recreational sports.

22/23 Funding: $5.5 Million

CERF (Continuing Education Funds)(opens in new window)

Continuing Education Operating Fund and is comprised of the campus-approved per unit fees collected from students enrolling in self-support course offerings through Regional & Continuing Education.

22/23 Funding: $9.5 Million

Fund: C3181

Campus Partners (PDF)

Calculated distribution of the fee revenue collected through Continuing Education (RCE), distributed back to the colleges and the Provost's office and used as a supplemental funding source to enhance self-support programs.

22/23 Funding:  Percentage of RCE Revenue

Fund: C4401

Parking(opens in new window)

Campus parking facilities are not supported by taxpayer dollar but are instead self-supporting. They are paid for by the users of the facilities through payment of parking fees.

22/23 Funding: $990,000

Housing(opens in new window)

Funds generated from student fees for residence hall living/learning experiences. Housing operations are considered self-supporting and not supported by taxpayer dollars.

22/23 Funding: $22.3 Million

Miscellaneous Trust Funds

 These trust funds include various quasi-self-support activities that operate in tangent with the CSU operating fund. They include separate revenue sources that are not related to state support or student fees.

Auxiliary Organization Funds

Associated Students (AS)(opens in new window)

Under a student government umbrella, operates the bookstore, dining services, student union and student programs.

22/23 Funding: $22.5 Million

University Foundation (UF)(opens in new window)

Responsible for solicitation, acceptance, and administration of gifts to the University.

22/23 Funding: $9 Million

Chico State Enterprises

Develops and manages grants and contracts, campus centers and programs, enterprise projects and provides financial administrative services to the University Foundation.

22/23 Funding: $45.2 Million
